# The 12 Principles of Animation

The 12 principles are the difference between a shape *moving* and a shape being *alive*. Use them as a diagnostic checklist, not a lecture: scan the list, find what's missing, add it.

## Quick checklist

1. Squash & Stretch
2. Anticipation
3. Staging
4. Straight-Ahead vs. Pose-to-Pose
5. Follow-Through & Overlapping Action
6. Slow In & Slow Out
7. Arcs
8. Secondary Action
9. Timing
10. Exaggeration
11. Solid Drawing
12. Appeal

For each principle — its definition, how to spot it's missing, and how to add it — see `references/twelve-principles.md`.

Most "this logo/shape feels dead" fixes come from three principles: **anticipation** (a wind-up before the move), **follow-through** (things settle after, not all on one frame), and **slow in/slow out** (which is really easing — see motion-easing).
